who am i?

I'm Anthony Chan. I am a native New Yorker, a second-year undergraduate computer science major, and someone who loves to take on academic challenges. From competing in my high school math team to collaborating with others on hackathon projects, I enjoy the intellectual challenge and the process of solving problems.
My initial exposure to computer science was building games and simple programs in NetLogo in high school, and creating web pages and my first portfolio for a web development class in college. While I didn't enter college as a computer science major, I gradually came to realize that it's a field that I wanted to pursue. From there, I explored the many facets of web development on my own, and created my first web application Music Mentor. Ever since, I sought to expose myself to other subfields of computer science and software development, from writing software for microprocessors to building Android apps in Java.
I am a strong believer in working hard and in learning by doing. I truly enjoy what I do.

projects i've done:

Music Mentor

This online tool will help users learn music theory in a fun and innovative way. The slick design of the web application makes learn the core concepts of music theory effortless. Users can enjoy the various functions the web app supports, such as converting between music scales and creating chords. The web application has a companion Messenger bot that identifies any scale the user requests, in addition to other chat features.

This is a personal project started during the winter break of '16-17 and finished in February '17.
Created with: JavaScript, Node.js, Python, Flask, HTML, CSS

CollegeCare

A web application created as part of a NY State Making College Possible Coding competition. The purpose of our application is to provide valuable resources, mentoring opportunities, and an open forum to aid students to find an affordable college education in New York State. We were placed top 9 in whole competition out of over 60 competitors.

This was a 2-person project done in February '17.
Team members: Shaan Sheikh
Created with: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Python, Flask, SQLite3


Zero Waste

A website created along the theme of helping the environment. Our site aims to educate and help consumers of electronics in NYC to reduce their electronic wastes to ideally "zero".

This was a hackathon project submitted to Social Hack Day in Janurary '17.
Team Members: Jon Reyes, Kitty Liu
Created with: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, d3.js


Exercise Counter

We wanted to use LittleBits to create a device that we may use in everyday life. We came up with the exercise counter, which uses an Arduino bit and some sensors to count the number of repetitions for a number of exercises, such as pushups and squats.

This was a hackathon project submitted to HACKfEAT in October '16.
Team Members: Zhengyang Liu
Created with: Arduino, LittleBits
