my lifestyle

Pianist | Runner

the full scoop:

I like to have balance in my life because it means I am fulfilling as many of my needs as possible. As a student and programmer, I spend most of my time learning computer science or applied math, and working on programming projects. I strongly believe in the balance of learning new things and applying what I've learned. I need both to progress and achieve my goals.
When I'm not working, I pursue my hobbies which are piano and fitness. Both supplement my lifestyle by giving me the added mental stimulation and physical activity (intense piano-playing can be like a workout sometimes!). Otherwise, I'd be spending time with family, relaxing with friends, discussing programming concepts with fellow programmers, and having my regular dose of Reddit, YouTube, and Facebook.

i'm a pianist:

I picked up piano during the end of my sophomore year in college because I've simply always wanted to play the piano. While I did most of my piano learning on my own with the help of online resources, I did benefit from the help of friends and a wonderful music teacher at Stony Brook. Every week, I make time for piano practice and I enjoy it so much. My current repertoire is a mix of classical and pop songs, although my main focus is classical music.
For those who's interested, my classical repertoire currently consists of:
•Beethoven's "Fur Elise"
•Beethoven's "Moonlight Sonata (Mvt.1)"
•Yiruma's "River Flow In You"

I am always working on a new piece in my free time. For now, the pieces I'm currently working on include several other classical pieces:
•Liszt's "Liebestraum"
•Mozart's "Fantasy in D minor"

staying active:

The first thing I do every morning is to put on my running shoes. I love staying active and go to the fitness center many times a week. Staying fit is very important for my health and it also is a great way to feel refreshed and energized to be productive!
Because I sit for so much of the day (coding, learning in class, or playing the piano), I value my time in the gym because it gets my blood flowing, heart pumping, and mind going. My usual activities in the gym include running, weight training, and martial arts.
My personal mantra is "always take the stairs"!
